Congratulations on your acceptance to the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS) at the University of Kansas! In this section you find helpful information about the resources and service available to you as an EECS student, as well as details regarding what is required of you as you start your EECS academic career.

Explore: Undergraduate Info - Graduate Info

EECS User Accounts

All EECS students have an EECS account, seperate from their KU account, that is used to access EECS specific resources and services, such as:

  • An EECS email account
  • Accessing EECS computers
  • Logging into the EECS website
Since you will need your EECS account login information for many EECS courses, it is important that you activate your EECS account prior to the start of you first EECS course.

This account is created automatically once you have been accepted into the EECS department, but must be activated in person at the EECS Help Desk located in the Computing Commons on the main floor of Eaton Hall.

Your EECS email address will be used by EECS faculty and staff to communicate with you. This will require you to either check it regularly using the EECS Webmail Client, or have it forwarded to another email account that you check regularly.

For additional information about you EECS account, including how to forward your EECS email, visit the EECS Wiki.

Explore: EECS Wiki - Facilities

Administrative Assistance

If you have a problem with or question about:

  • Enrollment and Advising
  • Events and Deadlines
  • Program Requirements
the EECS administrative staff will be able to assist you. Adminsitrative staff can be contacted by email or phone, and can be located in the EECS Office on the second floor of Eaton Hall 8am-4:30pm Monday through Friday during the Spring and Fall semesters.

Explore: Administrative Staff

Technical Assistance

If you have a problem with or a question about:

  • Loggin into EECS Computers
  • Printing from EECS Printers
  • Accessing the EECS cycle severs
first check the EECS Wiki for solutions to common problems, and if you don't find an answer there, submit a online help request. The EECS Technical Staff responds to help request 8am-5pm Monday through Friday.

Explore: EECS Wiki - Tech Help - Technical Staff
